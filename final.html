What is Fire Safety?

Fire safety involves precautions that are taken to prevent or reduce the likelihood of a fire starting, and to minimize damage in case a fire does occur. It's crucial for protecting lives and property.

Fire Safety Statistics

Did you know that over 1.3 million fires are reported in the U.S. each year? Awareness and preparation can save lives!

Fire Prevention Methods

Install Smoke Detectors

Ensure smoke detectors are installed in every room and regularly check their batteries. Early detection can save lives.

Cooking Safety

Never leave cooking unattended. Keep flammable materials away from heat sources.

Create an Escape Plan

Develop and practice a fire escape plan with your family. Know two ways out of every room!

Common Fire Safety Myths

Myth: You can use water to put out all fires.

Fact: Water can make some fires worse. Always use the appropriate extinguishing method.

Myth: Smoke alarms are only needed in bedrooms.

Fact: Smoke alarms should be installed on every level of your home, including the basement.

Myth: Fire extinguishers are only for professionals.

Fact: Everyone should know how to use a fire extinguisher. Training is essential!
